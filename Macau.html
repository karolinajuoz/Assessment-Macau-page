Macau, China

About Macau:

Macau is an independant region on the south coast of China, across the Pearl River Delta from HongKong. It was considered Portuguese territory until 1999, due to this, it reflects various cultural influences (famous for its blend of Portuguese and Chinese cultures) and is also widley known for its gambling industry. It is a good one day trip destination if you are in HongKong.

My experience:

During my visit to Macau, I was captivated by the blend of Portuguese and Chinese architecture, especially at the Ruins of St. Paul's. The atmosphere in "Senado Square" was truly unique, with its bustling markets and colorful decorations. One of my favorite moments there was taking a serene gondola ride at the Venetian Macao, which felt like a slice of European culture (like Venice), although I was in Asia. Trying out the famous egg tarts while watching the gorgeous sunset was the perfect end to my day-trip in Macau.
